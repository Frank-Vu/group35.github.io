AIMS

Our most important aim while creating this scheduling webapp is to improve every day work efficiency. We wanted an app that can improve users’ ability to plan, prioritize their work and perform them productively. To achieve this, we must first work on the most important feature that makes a scheduling app a scheduling app: the calendar. We must design a calendar that display accurate dates and time, including holidays and leap year dates. To improve useability, we will include a few easy-to-use tools such as “sticky notes” to write reminders on each date or time. The user will also have the option to customize the calendar with different border colors, text font style and, of course, the ability to fill each date or time with different colors depends on how important that date or time is.

Another reason for us to make this app is that we want to help workers to form teams and to keep track of each other’s work, making collaboration easy and accessible. For that, we planned to include the roster management feature. This feature will let workers form teams with each other, as well as synchronize everyone calendars into one. It will display the name of every member in the team for everyone, while whoever created the team is assigned as Manager by default and have the option to assign other members their own roles. The synchronized calendar will merge every team member’s shifts and marked dates and time into together, which will be applied for everyone in the team.

For this app to truly help with improving work efficiency, it needs to be able to automatically do simple tasks for the users. The synchronized calendar mentioned above will have a system that detects any conflicts between the team members’ shifts and automatically notify them about it. Additionally, the app will have a booking system which will offer the ‘customer’ to automatically book for them appointments with employees from different companies. After a request for an appointment, the app will communicate with the company side and notify the Manager of the team. From here, the Manager can choose to either assign an employee by themselves or tell the app to automatically choose an employee and an available time for that appointment.

ROLES

For this project, we have defined four roles for our team: Team Coordinator, Front-end Developer, Back-end Developer, and Team Assistant.

- The Team Coordinator, which is similar to the leader of team, is the one who overlooks the entire project and their team, assigns tasks accordingly to each member. He or she is also the one who arranges time for group meetings, makes sure that everyone knows exactly what to do and the project to be on schedule.

- The Front-end Developer is responsible for designing the overall layout of the webapp. He or she will be that one that create the interface, makes which part of the app does what, and determines the look of the webapp. Creating a slick and modern look for webapp is essential in attracting and keeping users to use the app.

- The Back-end Developer is the one who will be dealing with data and internal systems of the webapp. Unlike the Front-end who determines the look of the webapp, the Back-end builds and maintain mechanisms that process data and performs action on the webapp. He or she is usually involved in data, storage, security and many things that are hidden away from the user. It is extremely important to make sure the webapp works correctly at all times and maintaining that status can help with satisfying the users.

- Team Assistant’s job is to either assist other roles in their work and take some of their workload off or attend to menial tasks while letting the team’s members focus on their tasks. He or she can also can help the Team Coordinator in arranging group meetings and reminding team members of their tasks. Like in the name, the Team Assistant’s purpose is to ‘assist’ other roles in order to keeping everyone focused on the project and potentially speed up the process.

SCOPE AND LIMITS

At first, we were very ambitious about this project, were constantly talk to each other about what ideas to put into this webapp and how to implement them. But we soon realized our pile of ideas became too big, and the lack of resources, knowledge and especially time were against our ambition. Eventually, we have to limit our scope down and prioritize a few features that were deemed essential for the app. Here are the features that are currently in our scope:

- Calendar: The core of a scheduling webapp. The calendar will display accurate dates and times, including dates for leap years. From here, you can mark any dates and times with different colors.

- Role management: Users will be able to form teams with each other, and the one who created the team will automatically be assigned as the manager by default. The manager then can set roles for team members and also will be able to track the work progression of each person. Events, deadline, and team meetings dates will all be assigned by the manager and will be displayed on the calendar for members to see. Team members will have to ability to set their own work status such as Done, Working on it, or Stuck so the manager can understand their current situation.

- Synchronization: When a person is added to a team, they will have the option to let the app automatically synchronize his/her calendar with the rest of their team members’. The user will be able to put every team member’s work shift, marked dates and time, “sticky notes”, available time and many more into his/her calendar. If the app detects any conflicts, such as your marked date is the same as somebody else’s, it will notify the user and present a few suggestions for the user for how to solve them.

As mentioned above, we had to scrap a few ideas since we since we lack the resources, knowledge, and time for them. Here is the list of the ideas that are out-of-scope:

- Booking: We plan to have two version of the app: one for customer, and one for company. The customer version is for users to book an appointment with a company that also uses the webapp. The company side is the one with the features mentioned in the in-scope list and to receive appointment requests from customers.

- Email notification: The app will send emails to remind the user if an important date is nearby. It will notify the user 24 hours, 12 hours, and 3 hours before the marked time. This feature can be turned off at any time through the option menu.

- SMS notification: Works exactly the same way as Email Notification, except it is through phone messages. Again, this feature can be turned off through the option menu.

TESTING

We cannot construct a new web application due to a lack of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Therefore, we create a prototype and design the website's UX/UI. In the prototype, users are required to interact and accomplish certain tasks with the website, and then provide feedback following testing.

First, we will devise a strategy for recruiting several test users. For instance, we can survey a group of ten university students who will be entering the workforce shortly or a group of five teachers/professors at RMIT University who require a system that can automatically check their calendars and add tanks and appointments.

Secondly, during the test, users are requested to do certain tasks, such as writing a book, inviting people to an event, reviewing team assignments and responsibilities, etc. There will be a time limit of 8-10 minutes. Then, we capture the entirety of the user's interaction with the web application. The rationale for this is that we want to know how successful they are at achieving their goals and what deters them from making progress.

After all, we provide them with a feedback form so they can elaborate on the (in)convenience, goal misunderstanding, and development opportunities.

Finally, once we have the records and user input, we will create a report for analysis and evaluation. Then, designers and code developers will eliminate the inconvenience and enhance the quickest method for accomplishing goals in web applications.
